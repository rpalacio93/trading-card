Emperor Penguin

The emperor penguin is the largest penguin in the world, and can dive deeper than any other bird.

  • Scientific Name: Aptenodytes forsteri
  • Average Length: 3.8ft (116 cm)
  • Average Lifespan: 20 years
  • Habitat: Antarctic continent

The tallest and heaviest of all penguins, the emperor penguin is endemic to Antarctica. Among the 17 different species of penguins, they are the only species that breeds during the Antarctic winter.
